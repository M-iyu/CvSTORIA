TIMELINE (1906 — PRESENT)

1906

The Cavite State University traces its humble beginnings to 1906 when it was established as the Indang Intermediate School by the American Thomasites, who were teachers sent from the United States to help rebuild and modernize the Philippine public education system during the early American colonial period. The school started as a pioneer intermediate institution in Indang, Cavite, with Mr. C.E. Workman serving as its first principal. He was later succeeded by two other American principals, Mr. Henry Wise and Mr. Joseph Cocannouer (also spelled Coconower in some records). This early phase laid the foundation for what would grow into a leading institution focused on agriculture, science, technology, and education, aligning with the university’s long-term mandate to provide advanced instruction and responsive research and extension services for the development of Cavite and beyond.

1915

The institution that would eventually become Cavite State University began its transformation during the early American colonial period. In 1915, the school, then known as the Indang Intermediate School, appointed its first Filipino principal, Mariano Mondoñedo (sometimes referred to as Dr. Mariano Mondoñedo). During this time, intermediate classes continued while placing strong emphasis on vocational agriculture for boys and domestic science for girls, aligning with the practical education goals of the era.

1918

In 1918, the name of the school changed to Indang Farm School (also referred to as Indang Farming School). Enrollment grew notably, and the school site expanded through generous land donations from prominent citizens of Indang, including Don Francisco Ocampo and Don Severino de las Alas, who had served as Secretary of the Interior in Emilio Aguinaldo’s cabinet. These contributions helped develop the institution’s farming facilities and infrastructure.

1927

By 1927, under the leadership of Principal Simeon Madlangsakay, the school was renamed Indang Rural High School. It began offering secondary-level courses in vocational agriculture (as early as 1923 in some records) and Home Economics, further strengthening its focus on rural development and practical skills for agricultural livelihoods.

1958

In 1958, the institution was renamed Don Severino National Agricultural School through congressional action. This change honored the significant contributions and land donations of Don Severino de las Alas to the local community, the province of Cavite, and the nation as a whole.

1964

A major milestone occurred on June 18, 1964, when Republic Act No. 3917 converted the Don Severino National Agricultural School into a state college, known as Don Severino Agricultural College (DSAC). The law, authored by Representative Justiniano Montano Sr., authorized the institution to offer expanded programs, including baccalaureate degrees in agriculture, agricultural education, home technology, agricultural engineering, and other related courses, along with associate and postgraduate studies. Vicente G. Hicaro served as officer-in-charge during the early transition period.

1967

In 1967, Santiago M. Rolle was appointed as the first president of the college. This appointment marked the full operational establishment of DSAC as a higher education institution dedicated to advanced instruction in agriculture and related fields.

1971

In 1971, the Don Severino Agricultural College (DSAC) offered the Certificate in Technical Agriculture as a post-secondary program. This complemented the existing baccalaureate degrees in agriculture and agricultural education. In the same year, Mr. Vicente T. Pinazo became the second college president.

1983-1987

From 1983 to 1987, Dr. Ruperto S. Sangalang served as officer-in-charge and then as president of DSAC. He provided leadership during the preparatory stage toward university status and held the position for the longest tenure in the institution’s history, continuing well after the transition.

1988-1992

From 1988 to 1992, DSAC implemented the Institutional Development Assistance and Cooperation Project. This initiative strengthened the college’s instructional, research, and extension programs through partnerships, particularly with the University of the Philippines Los Baños, enhancing overall institutional capacity.

1993-1998

From 1993 to 1998, the institution continued its development efforts. On January 22, 1998, by virtue of Republic Act No. 8468, the Don Severino Agricultural College was converted into a state university and renamed Cavite State University (CvSU). Dr. Ruperto S. Sangalang became the first university president.

2001

In 2001, the Cavite City campus was launched to broaden access to higher education in the province. A memorandum was also signed for the planned establishment of the Carmona Campus in 2002. In addition, the Cavite College of Fisheries (CACOF) in Naic and the Cavite College of Arts and Trade (CCAT) in Rosario were integrated into CvSU through CHED Memorandum Order No. 27 Series of 2000. These became the CvSU College of Fisheries and the CvSU College of Arts and Trade, respectively, expanding the university’s program offerings and multi-campus system.

2002

In 2002, CvSU becoming the CvSU College of Fisheries and the College of Arts and Trade, respectively. This expansion significantly broadened the university’s program offerings in fisheries, arts, trades, and related fields while strengthening its multi-campus system across the province.

Satellites / Branch Campuses

Cavite State University (CvSU) operates 11 satellite campuses throughout the province, extending accessible education to local communities. Each campus offers specialized programs ranging from business and IT to industrial technology and hospitality.

Naic Campus

Originally the Cavite College of Fisheries (CACOF), established in 1961–1962. It was integrated into CvSU in 2001–2002. The campus specializes in fisheries, aquatic sciences, and related fields. It offers 7 college programs, a postgraduate course, and a Science High School (Grades 7–10). Located in Brgy. Bucana Malaki, Naic, Cavite.

CCAT Campus (Rosario)

Formerly Cavite College of Arts and Trade (CCAT), founded in 1969/1970 and integrated into CvSU in 2001–2002. It emphasizes technical-vocational education, teacher education, and industrial programs. The campus spans parts of Rosario and General Trias and offers strong engineering, education, and hospitality programs and a Laboratory/Science High School.

Carmona Campus

Established in 2002 through a Memorandum of Agreement with the LGU of Carmona. It provides accessible higher education in education, business, IT, computer science, and hospitality. The campus continues to grow with modern facilities and focuses on producing industry-ready graduates.

Imus Campus

Launched in 2003 as the College of Business and Entrepreneurship; became a full satellite campus in 2012. Located along Aguinaldo Highway in Palico IV, Imus City. It offers a wide range of programs in business, education, IT, psychology, and journalism.

Trece Martires Campus

Established in 2005 in Brgy. Osorio (now Gregorio), Trece Martires City. It started small with non-degree programs and has expanded to offer education, business, hospitality, IT, and other undergraduate degrees.

Silang Campus

Established in 2006 on land donated by the LGU of Silang. It includes education, business, IT, tourism, psychology, and a Laboratory/Science High School.

Tanza Campus

Established in 2007 and started with only 81 students. Located in Tanza, Cavite, it offers programs in education, business, hospitality, tourism, IT, and psychology.

Bacoor Campus

Established in 2008. Located in Soldiers Hills IV, Molino VI, Bacoor City. It offers programs in education, business, computer science, and criminology.

General Trias Campus

Established in 2012 along Arnaldo Highway. It offers relevant programs in education, business, hospitality, tourism, IT, office administration, and psychology.

Maragondon Campus

The newest among these, established in 2015. Located in Maragondon, Cavite. It primarily offers basic education (pre-elementary to Grade 10 | Laboratory/Science High School).
